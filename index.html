Getting there

Traveling by car

Salisbury is around a 2 and a half hour drive from New York City, and a 3 and a half hour drive from Boston. The wedding is on a holiday weekend. The good news: potentially an extra day to spend with everyone. The less-good news: there may be increased traffic depending on when you come in, so to be safe you may want to add some time to those estimates. Sorry!

Traveling by air

  • The closest airport is Hartford International Airport which is approximately an hour's drive from the Grey Cottage.
  • Albany International Airport is a 90 minutes drive from the Grey Cottage.
  • JFK and New York City, generally, are about 2 and a half hours away by car.

Other transport

The Wassaic Metro-North Station will get you within a 40 minute drive of the venue if you're looking to take a train up from New York City.
Amtrak has a station in Pittsfield, MA, that has service from Chicago to Boston; this is also about a 45 minute drive from the venue.

FAQ

  • What should I wear?

  • The welcome dinner and day-after brunch are casual outdoor affairs, so please feel free to dress comfortably. The dress code for the wedding itself is "field-festive" -- think semi-formal/cocktail attire compatible with the great outdoors (Jody, for example, will not be wearing high heels on the grass). Gil is wearing a kilt. You do you, but maybe bring a shawl or sweater for those brisk October evenings!

  • Am I invited to the welcome dinner?

  • Yes. Yes you are.


For us, this ceremony is a chance not only to "make it official," but to bring together and celebrate with our community of friends and family in a place that is very dear to us. Over the years we have accumulated more than enough goods and gear to make our home a full and happy one, and there is very little that we need. Our wedding venue is a ways off the beaten path, and we recognize that the time, effort, and expense of trekking out to Salisbury is not inconsiderable! What we want more than anything is to have everyone together to celebrate with us: please know that your being there at our wedding really is the most meaningful gift we could ask for. We hope you will be able to join us in October for food, fellowship, and dancing! If you *would* like to give a gift, we wanted to make it as simple as possible by sharing a list of items that we know would be cherished and put to good use wherever life takes us (especially in the coming years as we transition away from some of our IKEA furniture and post-college cookware to something a little more lasting).
